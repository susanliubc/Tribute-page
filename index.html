Zengqi Wang, "the last pure Chinese scholar"

A contemporary Chinese writer famous for short stories and essays


Zengqi Wang was interviewed at home

Zengqi Wang's life:

  • 1920 - Born on Gaoyou, China
  • 1939 - Enrolled into the National Southwestern Associated Univesity
  • 1940 - Tutored by famous writer Congwen Shen during university life, started writing and finished his first draft "Revenge"
  • 1944 - Became a highschool teacher in Kunming, where he met Songqing Shi, his future wife
  • 1947 - Published his first short story "Famous Poultry Farmer"
  • 1948 - Moved to Beijing and got a job at museum later this year
  • 1949 - Followed the Fourth Field Army to go to the southern China during civil cadre
  • 1950 - Relocated to Beijing and became an editor for several journals
  • 1956 - Adapted the story of "Fan Jin" for Peking opera, therefore gained reputations
  • 1958 - Was targeted as "Rightist" and subcequently exiled to Zhangjiakou to draw various potatoes for a local agricultural institution
  • 1962 - Worked as a professional screenwriter in Beijing Opera Theater
  • 1968 - Was summoned by Jiang Qing, the First Lady of China at that time, to adapt the story of "Shajiabang", one of the eight "revolutionary model operas"
  • 1979 - Restarted normal writing
  • 1997 - Passed away in Beijing

Since childhood, Zengqi Wang's life was never far away from wars and campaigns for political change. Experiencing poverty, hunger, unemployment, seperation, and despisement, he still remained an intact inner world that had been nourished by seeking and chrishing life's goodness. Unlike some of his peers, Zengqi always demonstrated a traditonal Chinese scholar's life attitude: calm, natrual, and happy-go-lucky. The Cultural Revolution destrucated traditional Chinese culture and values, thus directly led to the formation of at least three generation of inadequately civilized individuals. Wang, regarded as "the last pure Chinese scholar", and his rich in spirit life style are worthy of study, given the fact of the spiritual poverty pervasive among current Chinese intellectual life.

A series of short stories were deemed to be his apogees of writing. The character of his contemprary works portrayed the rural life lyrically. These works stimulate writers of root-searching literature in the mid- and late 1980s.

Wang had a grasp of advanced culinary skills and was considered as a gourmet. This enthusiasm is also revealed in many of his works, such as "Hometown Foods".
